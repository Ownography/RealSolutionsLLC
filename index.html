Our "justice" system

The current justice system the the furthest from the term "justice", the Federal charges and sentencing follow codes and rules that the Defense attorneys either know but don't tell the client... or that they simply don't know themselves because these days, most cases are handled like a "handshake deal" and the prosecutors and judges make money for locking people up. They are not motivated by the truth but by money. A good paralegal who has passion for justice will serve a convicted person better than most attorneys. Post-conviction relief is possible.

Over-sentencing

Many people believe there is no hope if they actually committed the crime. That is not true because many people that should receive 12- 24 months are sentenced to 5-10 years. That is merely one example. Unless the case is reviewed by a professional who specializes in postconviction relief, they will not know if they have been over-sentenced. Over-sentencing is something which can be corrected with post conviction motions. There is something which you MUST have in order to be accepted by me as a client. you MUST have a copy of the PSR (pre-sentence report).

  • Is there any chance of getting my sentence overturned?

    There is always a chance of identifying and pointing out to the court key errors in the case and getting the case overturned; but, it must be done on time and by following the rules of the court.

  • How long does a post-conviction process take?

    The time cannot be precisely predicted but an estimate is 6months to 1 year from the date of filing the proper motion (and response, if required) in court.

  • Are there more than one kind of post-conviction motion?

    Yes, the kind needed is determined by the particular attributes of the case and how soon after the conviction the errors are detected.

  • Is there anything which can increase the chances of my postconviction motion succeeded?

    The shorter the time between the conviction and the motion, improves the chances of success.

  • Is there any common error that applies to most any case?

    Yes, it is called "Ineffective assistance of counsel". It means that there are gross or obvious errors which the defense attorney should have detected and pointed out and failed to identify/present.
